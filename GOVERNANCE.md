# TRACE-AI Governance Model

TRACE-AI is a community-driven initiative to establish standards for transparent reporting in AI-enabled catalysis.

---

## Organizational Structure

### Core Maintainers
Core maintainers are responsible for:

- Strategic direction of TRACE-AI
- Final decisions on major changes
- Ensuring alignment with scientific and community goals

### Contributors
Contributors include:

- Researchers submitting improvements via pull requests
- Community members proposing ideas or reporting issues

---

## Decision Making

- Minor updates: handled through PR review and maintainer approval
- Major changes (e.g., checklist structure, schema updates):
  - Discussed via Issues or Discussions
  - May involve broader community input
  - Final decision by core maintainers

---

## Authorship and Attribution

TRACE-AI follows principles of:

- Transparent attribution of contributions
- Recognition of both individual and community efforts
- Alignment with academic authorship norms when applicable

---

## Versioning

- TRACE-AI resources are versioned
- Changes are documented in CHANGELOG.md
- Releases correspond to stable checkpoints of the framework

---

## Community Engagement

TRACE-AI encourages:

- Open discussion of standards
- Contributions from diverse domains (experiment, theory, AI)
- Iterative refinement based on real-world use

---

## Future Development

TRACE-AI will evolve to support:

- Multimodal data integration
- Autonomous and agentic workflows
- Benchmarking and validation tools
- Integration with knowledge graphs and digital labs

---

## Contact

For questions or collaboration, please open an issue or contact the maintainers.
